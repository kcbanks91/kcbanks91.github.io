Course: HCI 454 - Interaction Design and Information Architecture

Five Hat Racks

      information architecture

BACKGROUND

The concept of the "Five Hat Racks" was originally developed by Richard Saul Wurman in his book Information Anxiety. In a nutshell, there are 5 ways to organize information: By Location, Alphabet, Time, Category and Continuum. Every way you can think of to organize data will fit in to one of those groupings.

For this assignment, students were given direction to choose a subject of their choice and then:

     • Organize the subject using at least three of the five racks

     • Create a graphic that combined the hat racks and include an explanation of why you chose the topic and racks

DELIVERABLE

I decided to focus on the main character deaths that have occurred thus far in the Game of Thrones novels because it’s mainly these character deaths that have propelled the story forward.

I first chose “Location” because it brings Westeros' size into perspective and shows that there is no part of the kingdom that has been left untouched by death.

I then chose alphabetically by surname, as many times in the books characters are referred to by solely their surnames (as that is what matters most in Westeros.)

Lastly, I organized categorically by house, as this is very defining for characters. Their sigils and colors are described at every chance and the characters take great pride in their houses.
